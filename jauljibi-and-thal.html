Jauljibi And Thal

Description

  • This fair is held every year in November at Jauljibi, the confluence ofthe rivers Kali and Gori, which is a meeting place of three
    different cultures – the Shauka, the Nepali and the Kumaoni.
  • This gateway to Johar, Darma, Chaudans and Byans was at one time, considered to be the central place between Tibet and the Tarai regions.
  • Though this fair is primarily a commercial one, its cultural importance cannot be overlooked.
  • People come even from Nepal to this fair in order to sell horses, ghee and foreign goods and take back foodgrains, jaggery etc.
  • A similar fair is held at Thal on Vaishakh Sankranti (14 April) and it attracts a large number of Shaukas.
  • With the closure of the Indo – Tibet trade these fairs have lost their former importance.
